# Sports Raise Landing Page / Website Outline

> Working planning doc for the first Sports Raise marketing page. Updated from David's voice notes.

## 1. Strategic direction

The first Sports Raise landing page is for **campaign operators**: nonprofits, charitable organizers, and individuals raising money for a specific cause. The initial real-world users are likely people David personally knows who already participate in charitable activity and are willing to launch early campaigns.

Long-term, Sports Raise likely needs a bifurcated experience:

1. **Campaign operator experience** — start a campaign, seed prize pool, invite supporters, track impact.
2. **Fan contributor experience** — sports-prediction/game-day entertainment tied to donations, credits, rewards, and leaderboards.

These may eventually become two distinct site experiences or even two separate URLs. For now, v1 should focus on the operator/fundraiser page.

## 2. Core positioning

**Product summary:** Sports Raise helps fundraisers turn sports fans' desire for game-day action into repeat charitable contributions. Supporters donate to a campaign, receive non-cash credits, make picks or participate in sports challenges, compete for donated rewards, and see the impact of the campaign.

**Important public framing:** Donation-first and fan-powered. Do not use public-facing terms like “sportsbook” or “wagering operator.” Internally, sportsbook-like behavior explains the engagement loop, but public language should use:

- game-day action
- picks
- predictions
- challenges
- non-cash credits
- donated rewards
- prize pool
- leaderboards
- campaign play
- impact

## 3. Target audiences

### Primary audience for v1: campaign operators

Includes:
- Nonprofits
- Cause organizers
- Individuals raising for a particular cause
- Community sports families
- Local organizers
- People with an existing personal charitable mission

Needs:
- A new fundraising format that feels exciting and repeatable.
- A way to engage donors beyond a static donate button.
- A way to use sports moments, especially football season, to bring people back.
- Confidence that setup is simple and the prize/reward pool can be seeded.

Message:
> Give your supporters a reason to come back every game day.

### Secondary audience: fan contributors

Needs:
- Something riding on the game they are already watching.
- A fun alternative to giving money to major sportsbooks.
- A way to contribute to something meaningful while still participating in picks/challenges.

Message:
> Put your game-day energy toward something that matters.

### Partner/prize audience

Includes:
- Sponsors
- Local businesses
- Athletes
- Teams
- Collectors/memorabilia donors
- Campaign creators donating items
- Sports Raise seeding early rewards

Needs:
- Turn donated items, memorabilia, experiences, and local prizes into engagement.
- Create measurable community impact.

## 4. Urgency / launch context

David wants **5–10 campaigns live as soon as next week**, mostly with people he knows who are willing to try the concept.

Football is a strategic anchor:
- NFL and college football are the biggest North American sports wagering contexts.
- Even before the season starts, Sports Raise can create anticipation with campaign pages and upcoming football picks/challenges.
- Early games/challenges can be listed well before final closeout, giving time to refine game mechanics, odds/game APIs, scoring, and the fan experience.

Possible campaign teaser direction:
> Football season is coming. Start turning your supporters' game-day energy into impact now.

## 5. Landing page narrative

1. **Hero:** Campaign operators can turn sports fans' game-day action into charitable contributions.
2. **Problem:** People already want something riding on games; most of that energy flows to major sportsbooks instead of meaningful causes.
3. **Solution:** Create a Sports Raise campaign where supporters donate, receive credits, make picks, compete for rewards, and see impact.
4. **Why it repeats:** A game-driven format gives supporters reasons to come back and re-up throughout the season.
5. **Prize pool:** Campaign creators, local donors, sponsors, and Sports Raise can seed donated rewards, memorabilia, and experiences.
6. **Launch angle:** Bring 5–10 campaigns live quickly with early football-focused challenges.
7. **CTA:** Apply/join/start a pilot campaign.

### C. The insight / problem

Explain carefully:
- Sports fans already want action on games.
- Instead of that money flowing only to major sportsbooks, Sports Raise lets fundraisers redirect game-day energy toward causes.
- The entertainment loop creates a reason to contribute again.

Avoid implying gambling or cash-out betting. Use “action” carefully, paired with donation-first language.

### D. How it works

Four steps:
1. Start a campaign for your cause.
2. Seed rewards with donated items, sponsor perks, or Sports Raise starter prizes.
3. Invite supporters to donate and receive non-cash credits.
4. Run game-day picks, leaderboards, and rewards while tracking impact.

### E. Why fundraisers use it

- More repeat engagement than a static donation page.
- Built around sports moments people already care about.
- Prize pools can be donated or sponsored.
- Supporters can re-up throughout a season.
- Transparent impact keeps the campaign mission central.

- Build supporter lists and prize pools before the season starts.

## 7. Visual/design direction

**Do not use purple as a primary color.** David associates purple with generic AI slop.

David shared these references for the level of 3D polish / wow factor, not for content:
- https://dribbble.com/shots/26973886-3d-video-for-a-Cyber-Security-Ecosystem
- https://dribbble.com/shots/26371249-3D-Visuals-Animation-for-Websline-s-campaign

Reference-derived direction:
- High-end 3D/isometric ecosystem, not flat SaaS cards.
- Modular physical blocks/components connected by glowing paths.
- Soft global illumination, premium shadows, translucent/frosted materials, subtle internal glows, and light film grain.
- The visual should explain Sports Raise as a connected system: campaign setup → donation/credits → picks/challenges → prize pool → impact.
- Embedded 3D labels or physical signage should feel integrated into the objects rather than simple 2D overlays.
- Motion direction later: calm tactical camera movement, soft parallax, glowing path pulses, modules assembling/waking up.

Recommended palette direction:
- Deep navy / ink for trust and sports-tech credibility.
- Clean white or off-white background.
- Emerald/green for giving, impact, and successful campaign momentum.
- Electric blue or cobalt as a sports-tech/action accent.
- Gold/amber for rewards and prize moments.
- Optional dark stadium/field gradients for football section.

Potential tokens:
- Background: #ffffff / #f6f9fc
- Ink/navy: #061b31 or #071827
- Body slate: #64748d
- Primary action blue: #0f62fe or #1457d9
- Impact green: #15be53 or #0e9f6e
- Reward gold: #d99a2b
- Border: #e5edf5
- Dark section: #071827

Style:
- Premium sports-tech, not casino.
- Professional 3D hero with real visual drama.
- Fintech-level trust with sports energy.
- Avoid generic gradients and purple-heavy SaaS visuals.
